The Experience

Visitors don't press buttons or touch screens — they simply move, and the space comes alive around them. CEING uses MediaPipe's hand and pose tracking to capture the visitor's body in real-time, translating gestures into generative visuals and spatial audio.

Each interaction is unique. The visuals and sound evolve based on the speed, position, and quality of movement. Slow, deliberate gestures produce different responses than energetic, expansive ones. The system responds to presence, not commands.

Technical Approach

CEING runs entirely in the browser — no app download, no installation, no special hardware. This was a deliberate design decision: the experience should be accessible to anyone with a camera and a screen.

MediaPipe

Real-time hand and pose tracking via webcam. No external sensors needed.

Web Audio API

Spatial audio synthesis driven by body position and gesture velocity.

WebGL

GPU-accelerated generative visuals. Particles, trails, and feedback loops.

Zero Install

Works in any modern browser. Scalable from phone to projection wall.

Why Browser-Based?

Most interactive installations require specialized hardware and software. CEING proves that compelling interactive experiences can run in a standard web browser. This matters for scalability: the same experience can run on a phone, a laptop, or a 4K projection wall without modification.

For commercial spaces, this means lower deployment cost, easier maintenance, and the ability to update content remotely. For art contexts, it means anyone can experience the work from anywhere.

TouchDesigner Integration

While the web version is self-contained, CEING also bridges to TouchDesigner via OSC for higher-fidelity installations. The browser handles tracking; TD handles the visuals at full resolution with multi-projector output.
