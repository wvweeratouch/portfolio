The Four Principles

Pichet Klunchun's No.60 methodology decodes Thai classical dance into fundamental movement principles. Khwan Dance translates these principles into a computational system that responds to the dancer in real-time.

  • Focal Points — Where the body directs attention. Tracked via skeleton joint analysis.
  • Focused Energies — Energy circulates, never accumulates. Mapped through movement velocity and acceleration.
  • Lines Becoming Curves — Straight lines dissolve into curves. Tracked via joint trajectory trails.
  • Awareness / Consciousness — Sustained presence throughout movement. Detected through consistency and smoothness filters.

Technical System

Kinect body tracking captures the dancer's skeleton in real-time. The four principles are mapped to CHOPs (Channel Operators) in TouchDesigner, where each principle generates a distinct visual layer that responds to the dancer's movement quality.

Kinect Tracking

Full body skeleton capture. Joint positions, velocities, accelerations.

TouchDesigner CHOPs

Each principle mapped to channel operators. Real-time analysis pipeline.

Generative Visuals

Four visual layers responding to four principles. The dance creates the visuals.

Live Performance

Designed for live touring. Robust enough for stage conditions.

International Tour

Khwan Dance toured with the Asia Culture Center in Gwangju, Korea, bringing Thai classical dance tradition into dialogue with contemporary interactive technology on an international stage.

The project demonstrates that traditional art forms and cutting-edge technology aren't in opposition — they can amplify each other. The dancer's centuries-old technique becomes more visible, not less, when translated into light and motion.
