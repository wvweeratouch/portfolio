The Project

Technobiological Futures Co-Laboratories is an international research initiative led by Marc Dusseiller (Hackteria/SGMK, Switzerland) and funded by the Pro Helvetia Synergies Fund. The project brings together artists, scientists, and makers across four countries to explore how biology and technology can co-create new forms of knowledge and artistic practice.

I serve as a core team member and Chiang Mai chapter lead, coordinating local workshops, research activities, and community engagement in northern Thailand.

Research Themes

  • Quantum Ceremonies — Ritual and quantum phenomena as artistic metaphor
  • Living Code — Programming with biological systems and processes
  • Genetic Weaving — Textile arts meet genetic data visualization

Physical AI: My Cross-Cutting Method

My contribution centers on Physical AI — using TinyML (machine learning on microcontrollers) to create intelligent sensors that can be deployed in biological and environmental contexts.

This approach bridges the gap between digital intelligence and physical biology. An ESP32 microcontroller running a trained ML model can classify plant electrical signals, detect fungal growth patterns, or analyze biorhythms — all locally, without cloud dependency, using open hardware.

Consortium

The project operates as a distributed consortium with chapters in:

  • Switzerland — Hackteria/SGMK (project lead)
  • Thailand (Chiang Mai) — Wave, Bora Hong, Bos, Nalika
  • Indonesia (Bali) — Connected to GOSH10 conference (Oct 2026)
  • India (Bangalore) — Yashas Shetty, art-science research

The project unfolds over four phases with bi-monthly consortium meetings, culminating in a final exhibition and publication in 2027.
