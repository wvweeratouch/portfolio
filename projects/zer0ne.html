Gaze as Interface

Most interactive art responds to body movement — where you stand, how you gesture. ZER0NE pushed into new territory: machine vision systems that respond not just to body movement but to where you look.

Eye tracking transforms the act of looking into an active input. Your attention becomes a cursor, your gaze becomes interaction. The work questions how technology perceives and responds to human consciousness.

Robotics Integration

The residency combined eye tracking with robotic systems that physically respond to the viewer's gaze. When you look at the work, it looks back. The relationship between viewer and artwork becomes reciprocal — both are observing, both are responding.

Eye Tracking

Gaze detection and attention mapping. Where you look drives the system.

Robotics

TouchDesigner

About ZER0NE

ZER0NE is a Korean art-tech residency program that brings together artists and technologists to create new work at the intersection of art and emerging technology. Artists are selected by jury based on their proposal and portfolio.

Being selected for ZER0NE provided dedicated time, resources, and a community of peers to push into experimental territory that wouldn't be possible in a commercial context.
